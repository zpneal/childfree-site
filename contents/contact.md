### Dr. Zachary Neal
Dr. Zachary Neal is a [Professor of Psychology at Michigan State University](https://psychology.msu.edu/directory/neal-zachary.html). He is the author of four books and over 100 peer-reviewed articles, and serves as the editor of the journal [_Global Networks_](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/14710374). In addition to his research on childfree adults, he also studies social networks, where he is focused on developing statistical methods and software that make complex networks easier to understand. When he's not working on research, he loves to travel, especially to regions known for their wine.

You can find out more about his other research at https://www.zacharyneal.com, and you can contact him at [zpneal@msu.edu](mailto:zpneal@msu.edu).

### Dr. Jennifer Watling Neal
Dr. Jennifer Watling Neal is a [Professor of Psychology at Michigan State University](https://psychology.msu.edu/directory/watlingneal-jennifer.html). She is the author of over 90 peer-reviewed articles, and serves as the editor of the journal [_Social Development_](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/14679507). In addition to her research on childfree adults, she also studies social networks in educational contexts, where she is focused on how children's peer networks shape their behavior and well-being and how teachers' networks facilitate the adoption and use of new programs and practices. When she's not working on research, she loves spoiling her two cats, traveling the world, and listening to obscure indiepop music.

You can find out more about her other research at https://www.jennawneal.com, and you can contact her at [jneal@msu.edu](mailto:jneal@msu.edu).

### MSU Childfree Research Fund
The Michigan State University Childfree Research Fund supports empirical research about childfree adults around the world. Because MSU is a registered 501(c)(3) organization, donations to this fund may be tax deductible; all donors receive a tax receipt for tax purposes. Donations are used to collect data, analyze results, and publish findings. All data and publications supported by the fund are freely available on the resources page.
